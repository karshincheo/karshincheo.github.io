I spent the first part of my career making things precise: financial models, value-creation plans, risk frameworks. Precision is a real skill, and I'm glad I have it. But the work that taught me the most always started messy — a half-built prototype in front of a real person, watching where they got confused.

This page is where I'll write about that: building products, talking to customers, and the gap between a clean strategy and a thing that actually works in someone's hands.

A few things I keep coming back to:

- **Discovery beats sizing.** It's easier to find a problem people feel than to size a market on a spreadsheet. The interview tells you more than the TAM.
- **Ship to learn.** A rough product in front of ten real users is worth more than a perfect deck in front of none.
- **Taste compounds.** The hundredth thing you build is better than the first, and not by a little.
